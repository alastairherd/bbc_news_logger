# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Researchers and journalists investigating how BBC front-page promotion relates to most-read rankings over time. They need reproducible filters, source links, precise timestamps, and downloadable evidence.

## Product Purpose

Maintain a reliable public archive of BBC News observations and expose it as a fast research dashboard, queryable dataset, and bounded set of AI and MCP tools. Success means the archive remains current without growing the code repository, and every visible claim can be traced to captured observations.

## Brand Personality

Technical, rigorous, and calm. The interface should feel like a well-made data instrument rather than a newsroom imitation or a chat product.

## Anti-references

Avoid terminal overload: monospace everywhere, faux command prompts, dense unstructured logs, or controls that assume developer knowledge. Also avoid BBC brand imitation and generic SaaS metric-card layouts.

## Design Principles

- Evidence stays close to every chart and answer.
- Filters are shareable, predictable, and reversible.
- The static research experience remains useful when AI is unavailable.
- Dense information uses hierarchy and alignment rather than decoration.
- External-service limits are visible and fail gracefully.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort accessible implementation with keyboard-operable controls, visible focus, contrast-safe colors, reduced-motion handling, semantic tables, and useful alternatives to chart-only information.
